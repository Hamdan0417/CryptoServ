Crypto Serv – Expanded Business Plan & Whitepaper (v1.1)

Planned Start Date: 15 November 2025

Scope: A complete, end-to-end plan covering strategy, product, technology, security, legal/compliance, operations, organization, finance, and a precise roadmap with dates and gates, plus an updated whitepaper.

1) Executive Summary

Vision: Build the most trusted, integrated service infrastructure for builders, companies, and investors in digital assets.

Mission: Operate a unified services platform backed by the SERV token, delivering specialist hiring, quality assurance and certifications, advisory, licensing support, project rescue, listing facilitation, and a loyalty/rewards program.

Value Proposition: Reduce market fragmentation with an auditable, standards-driven marketplace and service stack that improves trust, speed, and outcomes.

Key 12–18-Month Targets

  • TGE + first DEX listing in Q3–Q4 2026.
  • MVP shipping: (1) Specialist Jobs Marketplace, (2) Loyalty & Airdrops, (3) Advisory.
  • Reach 25k registered users, 3k active candidates, 500 hiring companies, 50 projects assessed under the certification program, and ≥ 2.5m SERV initial staking TVL.
  • Secure first-line partners: security auditor, KYC/AML provider, and Travel Rule provider.

2) Market & Competition

Customer Segments

  • Web3/Blockchain projects: need hiring, quality signals, listings, licenses.
  • Blockchain professionals: developers, security engineers, product managers, compliance.
  • Investors/incubators/VCs: validated deal flow and standardized diligence.
  • Institutional partners: auditors, payment providers, banks/EMIs.

Gaps & Pain Points: talent scarcity and matching, trust deficit, regulatory complexity, fragmented service providers, high risk exposure.

Competitive Landscape (high-level): generic job boards, tech consultancies, incubators, auditors, listing brokers. Crypto Serv integrates these into a single, measurable, token-aligned ecosystem with governance.

3) Business Model & Monetization

Revenue Streams

  1. Employer subscriptions (Basic/Pro/Enterprise) + paid job promotions.
  2. Take-rate (10–20%) on advisory/contract volume facilitated by the platform.
  3. Certification fees for Crypto Serv Quality Mark (CSQM) (tiered by project size/risk).
  4. Licensing/Compliance enablement (fixed + success fee).
  5. Listing facilitation (retainer + success).
  6. Staking/Treasury yields earmarked to ecosystem grants per policy.
  7. Advertising/placement (CPC/CPI/CPA) with strict quality filters.
  8. Card program revenue share (interchange/rebate) post-launch via licensed partners.

Indicative Pricing (subject to market test)

  • Employer subs: $199 / $499 / $1,999 monthly.
  • CSQM certification: $5k–$50k per project based on scope/risk.
  • Licensing/Compliance packs: $20k–$100k + success fee.
  • Advisory marketplace: 15% take-rate.

4) Corporate Governance & Legal Structure

Jurisdiction Strategy (by activity)

  • UAE: ADGM (Abu Dhabi) or VARA (Dubai) for virtual asset service activities (non-custodial advisory/brokerage elements).
  • Bahrain: CBB sandbox or VA provider categories.
  • Singapore: MAS/PSA for non-custodial intermediation/tech services.
  • Saudi Arabia: comply with E-commerce and PDPL; avoid regulated VA activities domestically until a clear framework is available.

Entity Stack: HoldCo (BVI/ADGM) + OpCo (ADGM/VARA) + Services/Advisory branch (KSA) + BD entities (Bahrain/Singapore) + future DAO Foundation.

Licensing Posture: begin non-custodial; no exchange/broker-dealer operation by Crypto Serv unless through licensed partners.

Internal Policies: Board charter, conflicts of interest, insider trading, gifts/hospitality, Risk & Compliance Committee charters.

5) Compliance & Regulatory Governance

  • KYC/KYB via third-party (e.g., SumSub/Onfido/Trulioo) with risk scoring.
  • AML/CFT risk-based program, sanctions screening, transaction monitoring, SAR/STR processes aligned with FATF.
  • Travel Rule integrations (e.g., Notabene/TRISA) where applicable.
  • Banking/fiat rails: only via licensed partners; segregate client vs treasury funds.
  • Privacy: GDPR, Saudi PDPL, DIFC DP Law; DPA & SCCs with processors; data retention and minimization.
  • Terms/Privacy: jurisdiction-aware ToS and Privacy Policy; risk disclosures; geofencing restricted jurisdictions.
  • Token classification: Utility token with Howey/MiCA/MAS/SFC analysis; avoid profit promises; separate future DAO governance from financial return language.
  • Offering structure: SAFT for accredited/eligible investors (e.g., Reg S/Reg D); exclude high-risk jurisdictions.
  • Appoint MLRO, DPO, and local regulatory counsel.

6) Technical Plan (Architecture & SDLC)

Chain selection: multi-chain strategy starting with EVM. Compare BNB / Polygon / Base / Arbitrum.

  • Primary criterion: low fees, strong security, toolchain maturity, liquidity, partner ecosystem.
  • Final decision by Week 6 using a transparent Decision Matrix.

Smart Contracts

  • Token: ERC-20 (optionally ERC-20Votes later).
  • Patterns: UUPS/Transparent Proxy, AccessControl, Pausable, ReentrancyGuard.
  • Modules: Treasury, Staking, Airdrop, Vesting, Governance.
  • Testing: unit + property-based; coverage ≥95%; fuzzing and economic sims.

Backend

  • Microservices (Node.js/TypeScript) behind a GraphQL gateway.
  • Data: PostgreSQL (OLTP), Redis (cache/queues), Elasticsearch (search).
  • Storage: S3-compatible with server-side encryption.
  • Messaging: Kafka/RabbitMQ (events for KYC, hiring, contracts).
  • Identity & Access: OIDC (Keycloak/Auth0).

Frontend & UX

  • Web (Next.js/React) + Mobile (React Native), i18n (EN/AR).
  • Accessible Design System (WCAG 2.1 AA).

DevSecOps/SRE

  • Infra: managed Kubernetes (EKS/AKS/GKE) + IaC (Terraform) + GitHub Actions.
  • Security: SAST/DAST, dependency scanning, SBOM, image signing (Cosign), HSM/MPC for sensitive keys.
  • Observability: OpenTelemetry, Prometheus, Grafana; SLOs (public platform 99.9% availability); centralized logging.
  • Backup/DR: RPO ≤ 1h, RTO ≤ 4h; quarterly DR tests.

Security by Design

  • External audits (CertiK/Trail of Bits/Quantstamp), bug bounty (Immunefi).
  • Stress tests against MEV/Sybil exploitation for airdrops.
  • Treasury keying via Gnosis Safe 3-of-5 and/or MPC (Fireblocks/BitGo) per policy.

7) Product Design (MVP → V1)

MVP scope

  1. Specialist Jobs Marketplace
    • Verifiable talent profiles (GitHub/API checks; on-chain skill badges).
    • Job board + rule-based/ML matching.
    • Employer accounts, billing, subscriptions.
  2. Loyalty & Airdrops
    • Points accrual from staking and platform activity.
    • Anti-Sybil: optional higher-tier KYC, one-wallet-per-user, reward caps.
  3. Advisory Marketplace
    • Expert network (Legal/Tech/Tokenomics/BD); booking; standard contracts; escrow via licensed partner.

Beta (Q2–Q3 2026):

  • CSQM certification levels (Bronze/Silver/Gold).
  • Certification console: intake → documentation → technical review → leadership interviews → final report + CSQM level → 90-day follow-up.

V1 (Q4 2026–Q1 2027):

  • SERV listed on DEX + initial CEX partnerships.
  • Mobile apps GA.
  • Licensing/compliance services expansion; distressed-project rescue offering.

8) Tokenomics (Refined)

Name: Crypto Serv Token (SERV)

Standard: ERC-20 (EVM)

Max Supply: 1,000,000,000 SERV (fixed)

Allocation (revised)

  • Public/Initial Sales: 20%
  • Ecosystem & Rewards (staking/loyalty): 32%
  • Team: 12% (12-month cliff; 24-month linear vest)
  • Advisors: 3% (6-month cliff; 18-month linear vest)
  • Treasury/Strategic Partnerships: 18%
  • Marketing & Growth: 10%
  • Liquidity & Listings: 5%

Treasury Policy:

  • Initial DEX liquidity target: 5–10% of raise (50/50 pools).
  • Diversify treasury (stablecoins/BTC/ETH) with risk limits; sub-treasury committee.

Utility:

  • Fee discounts (up to 30%) when paying platform fees in SERV.
  • Staking to earn loyalty points convertible to airdrops/discounts.
  • Tiered access for premium jobs, fast-track certifications, investor rooms.
  • Future governance (DAO) on parameters (discounts, grants, burn ratios).

Supply Discipline: no post-mint inflation; optional fee-burn (up to 20% of net platform fees) subject to DAO vote.

12) Org Design & Internal Governance

Core Team: CEO, CTO, CPO, CFO, GC/Head of Legal, VP Compliance/MLRO, VP Engineering, Head of Security, Head of Product, Head of BD/Partnerships, Head of Marketing, Head of Community, Head of Customer Success.

Committees: Risk & Compliance, Information Security, Treasury.

13) Operational Runbooks

  • User/Company Onboarding: verification → ToS acceptance → risk assessment → tiering.
  • CSQM Certification: intake → documentary review → technical due diligence → leadership interviews → scoring → issuance → 90-day check.
  • Distressed Project Rescue: 2–4-week diagnosis; 90-day plan with OKRs; interim governance if needed.
  • Exchange Listings: DD pack, AML screens, liquidity coordination, comms plan.
  • Incident Response: P0/P1/P2 classification; war-room channels; P0 ≤ 15 min response; post-mortem within 72h.

14) Risk Management

Categories: regulatory, contractual/legal, cyber, operational, reputational, market/liquidity, smart-contract, fraud/Sybil, third-party.

Controls: strong access controls, 4-eyes for treasury, professional/management liability cover, semi-annual pen-testing, DEX liquidity exposure limits, strict environment separation.

Risk Appetite: probability/impact matrix, escalation thresholds, board-approved tolerances.

Whitepaper – v1.1

0) Disclaimer

Informational only; not investment, legal, or financial advice. Jurisdictional restrictions may apply.

1) Introduction

Crypto Serv addresses crypto service fragmentation through a unified platform powered by SERV, embedding measurable quality standards, loyalty mechanics, and verifiable talent/advisory matching.

2) Problem

Shortage of specialist talent, trust deficit, licensing complexity, and multi-vendor overhead increase cost, time, and risk.

3) Solution

An integrated services marketplace + CSQM quality mark + expert network + loyalty + listing/licensing facilitation under transparent governance.

4) Token Design

  • Supply: 1,000,000,000 SERV (fixed).
  • Allocation: as in Section 8.
  • Utility: discounts, staking, tiered access, future governance.
  • Discipline: optional fee-burn from platform revenues.

5) Architecture

Upgradeable contracts with multi-sig governance; high test coverage; third-party audits; bug bounty. Scalable backend with observability, privacy and security controls by default.

6) Security & Compliance

Risk-based AML/KYC, Travel Rule integrations, geo-blocks, privacy programs, appointed MLRO/DPO.

7) Roadmap

Q4 2025–Q1 2026: foundation and core tech. Q2–Q3 2026: MVP/Beta + partnerships. Q3–Q4 2026: TGE + listings + V1 launch. 2027: services expansion + geo expansion; 2028+: DAO/own-chain research.

8) Team & Advisors

To be announced at public launch (with background checks, conflict-of-interest commitments, track records).

9) Legal Annex

SAFT/ToS/Privacy/SLA/Arbitration; Howey/MiCA analyses; responsible marketing guidance.
